About Me

Since I was small, I had always been interested in robots, and how they learned to mimic human behaviour. I had the opportunity to see ASIMO in person at the Miraikan in Tokyo, and watched the robot perform a backflip, and score a soccer ball. It amazed me how people were able to recreate human-like joints and limbs out of metal, wires, and plastic.
Seeing ASIMO in action sparked an interest for robotics in me, and engineering seemed like the obvious choice for me to make to learn more about the field. However, I soon learned that engineering is more than just physics and calculation; it's a way of thinking, and a way of objectively looking at a problem to come up with the best solution.
In the future I hope to specialize in mechatronics, developing integrated robotic mechanisms to help enhance human life.

Projects

Battery Bay Design

Robotics for Space Exploration

During first year, I created a battery and circuit panel mount for the Robotics for Space Exploration design team’s 2019-2020 Mars Rover. In this project, I was required to communicate with both the mechanical and electrical sub-teams to design a system that was compact, robust, and allowed for quick battery exchanges during competitions.

Gearbox

MIE243: Mechanical Engineering Design

In MIE243, our 2nd year mechanical engineering design course, we designed gearboxes in groups of 4 to apply the knowledge we had acquired in class. We were required to design gearboxes which were completely 3D printed under 8 hours; they were 3D printed in the University of Toronto mechanical engineering facilities and assembled by a teaching assistant; w. This gearbox has a 16:1 reduction, and has a colinear input and output.

LED Sponsor Board

FRC Team 6135: Arctos

In grade 12, I designed a LED sponsor board for use in our robotics team’s displays at competitions. The LED light base was purchased, and the panel’s design was done on Illustrator; it includes all of our team’s high level sponsors, and utilizes the bear motif to be consistent with the branding expectations. The acrylic panel was laser etched and cut by a local sponsor.
